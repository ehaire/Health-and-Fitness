Legs
Legs Squat 2–3 × 10 ▼
Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, toes pointed slightly out. Push your hips back and bend your knees, lowering until your thighs are as close to parallel to the floor as comfortable. Keep your chest up and knees tracking over your toes. Push through your heels to stand back up.
Progression Start by squatting down to touch the ottoman and standing back up. If needed, sit fully and use your hands to help get up. Work toward just touching it lightly and standing without sitting.
Legs Reverse Lunge 2–3 × 8 each ▼
Stand tall, feet together. Step one foot back and lower your back knee toward the floor, keeping your front knee directly over your ankle. Push through your front heel to step back to standing. Alternate legs each rep. Hold a chair or wall for balance if needed.
Why reverse instead of forward Stepping back is easier on the knees and requires less balance than a standard forward lunge.
Glutes & Hips
Glutes Hip Thrust 2–3 × 12 ▼
Sit on the floor with your upper back resting against the couch cushion, knees bent, feet flat on the floor. Drive through your heels and squeeze your glutes to lift your hips until your body forms a straight line from shoulders to knees. Hold for a second at the top, then lower back down slowly.
Back
Back Single Arm Dumbbell Row 2–3 × 10 each ▼
Place one hand and the same-side knee on a chair or couch for support. Hold a dumbbell in the opposite hand, arm hanging straight down. Pull the dumbbell up toward your hip — not your shoulder, so your hand ends up roughly at your waistline when your elbow is fully bent. Keep your elbow close to your body and your back flat throughout. Lower slowly.
Chest
Chest Wall Push-Up 2–3 × 10–15 ▼
Stand facing a wall, arms extended, hands flat on the wall at shoulder height. Bend your elbows and lower your chest toward the wall, then push back to the start. The further your feet are from the wall, the harder it is.
Progression Wall push-ups → hands on a countertop → knee push-ups on the floor.
Shoulders
Shoulders Seated Dumbbell Press 2–3 × 10 ▼
Sit in a chair with back support. Hold a dumbbell in each hand at shoulder height, palms facing forward. Press both dumbbells straight up until your arms are almost fully extended, then lower back to shoulder height slowly.
Arms
Biceps Dumbbell Curl 2–3 × 10–12 ▼
Stand or sit with a dumbbell in each hand, palms facing forward, arms at your sides. Curl both dumbbells up toward your shoulders by bending at the elbow, keeping your upper arms still. Lower slowly.
Triceps Dumbbell Kickback 2–3 × 10–12 ▼
Hinge forward slightly at the hips, bracing your free hand on a chair. Hold a dumbbell with your upper arm pinned against your side, elbow bent at 90°. Straighten your arm back and up by extending the elbow until your arm is straight, then slowly return. Keep your upper arm still the whole time.
Core
Core Dead Bug 2 × 8 each side ▼
Lie on your back, arms pointing straight up toward the ceiling, knees bent at 90° in the air. Slowly lower one arm back overhead and extend the opposite leg out, keeping your lower back pressed into the floor. Bring them back and switch sides. Move slowly and controlled.
Why this over crunches No strain on the neck or spine. Works deep core stability.
General guidelines
Rest 60–90 seconds between sets. Start with light dumbbells (5–8 lbs) and only add weight when the last few reps feel easy. It's okay to do fewer sets at first — 2 sets of everything is plenty to start. The goal right now is learning the movements and building the habit.
